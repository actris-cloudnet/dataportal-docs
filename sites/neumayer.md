# Neumayer

The Meteorological Observatory Neumayer has been part of all German research
stations on Ekström Ice Shelf, Atka Bay, Antarctica since 1981. The first
station "Georg von Neumayer" had to be replaced by "Neumayer Station" in 1992,
which again was replaced in 2009 by "Neumayer Station III". Continuous
long-term atmospheric observations cover various parameters and degrees of
complexity: from basis synoptical observations over BSRN surface radiation
measurements, radiosoundings including in-situ ozone profiling, to remote
sensing instrumentation.

Remote sensing of cloud parameters started in 1992 with a ceilometer
Impulsphysik LD-WH X 05. The ceilometer has been replaced by Vaisala CL31 and
CL51 later. In 2019 a METEK Micro Rain Radar was installed. In 2023 TROPOS
Leipzig had a full-featured Cloudnet container running at Neumayer Station for
one year. In 2024 the remote sensing capabilities of the Meteorological
Observatory Neumayer were markedly extended by an RPG-FMCW-94 cloud radar,
MiniMPL lidar and HALO StreamLine XR+ Doppler lidar. Finally, in 2026 a RPG
HATPRO microwave radiometer was installed by AWI to replace the TROPOS
instrument that had been running there until then.
