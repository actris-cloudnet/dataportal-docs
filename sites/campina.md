# ATTO-Campina

The ATTO-Campina site is a permanent observational facility located in the
central Amazon, approximately 4 km from the Amazon Tall Tower Observatory
(ATTO), within the Uatumã Sustainable Development Reserve. Located 150 km from
the Manaus city (the Amazonas state capital), ATTO-Campina is installed in a
campinarana ecosystem characterized by sandy soils and relatively low
vegetation, thus providing favorable conditions for atmospheric remote sensing
observations that are not feasible under the dense forest canopy at the ATTO
tall tower site.

Operational since 2020, ATTO-Campina was designed to complement the in situ
measurements performed at the ATTO towers by extending observations to the full
atmospheric column, particularly focusing on cloud and precipitation processes.
Together, these sites form an integrated framework to investigate the complex
interactions between the rainforest and the atmosphere, especially those
involving gas, aerosol, cloud, and precipitation dynamics in a near-pristine
tropical environment.

ATTO-Campina is equipped with a comprehensive suite of remote sensing and in
situ instruments that provide continuous, high-resolution measurements from the
surface to the upper troposphere. These include cloud and wind profiling
radars, disdrometers for raindrop size distribution, a microwave radiometer for
temperature and humidity profiling, and a ceilometer for boundary layer and
cloud base detection. Additional measurements include atmospheric electric
field, integrated water vapor from a photometer and a GNSS receptor, standard
meteorological variables, and aerosol properties such as particle size
distributions. A flux tower complements these observations by measuring
exchanges of heat and moisture, longside soil temperature in different depths.
This diverse instrumentation enables detailed characterization of the vertical
structure of clouds, precipitation, and atmospheric state variables, supporting
advanced studies on gas–aerosol–cloud–precipitation interactions and their role
in the Amazon climate system.
